instructions
  • Catching and Throwing
    Rules:
    • Have a drink in hand Don’t argue - disputes are settled by cheers. Have a drink and play on.
    • Set your drink down? Have a drink!
    • Throw the Cork from a seated position.
    • Thrower’s hand can’t pass the cup line if so, draw a penalty card.
    • Any throw that hits the “Penalty Zone” draws a penalty card.
    • Corks must be caught by the other team before hitting the floor. If no catch, catching team drinks.
    • Clawbacks - If the cork is thrown and hits the cup and bounces ANYWHERE, the cork is live. Scramble and get that cork. Throwing team gets cork, whoever recovered it gets a free throw. Catching team recovers cork, play on.
    Rules:
    • Compound, don’t suck or you’re doomed.
    • Must be completed. If the penalty is not completed or attempted, finish your drink and draw another penalty card.
  • Game Play

    Ideally, Corkcup is played at a common dinner table with each team lined up, sitting at opposite ends.

    Each team will place their cup approximately 1 ft from the edge of the table, as a target for the other team

  • Items Required
    Standard Items:
    • 2 Coffee Mugs (smaller = more difficult)
    • 1 wine cork
    • 2-10 people
    Additonal items for penalties:
    • cereal bowl
    • shot glass
    • spoon
    • tongs
    Scoring:

    Games typically go to 3 or 5 but that's entirely up to the people playing
